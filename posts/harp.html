The Harp

Definition

The harp is a stringed instrument that produces sound by plucking its strings. It has a triangular frame and multiple strings of varying lengths that create a wide range of pitches.

History

The harp dates back thousands of years, with early versions found in ancient Mesopotamia and Egypt. It has been a significant instrument in folk and classical traditions worldwide.

How to Use

Harpists pluck the strings with their fingers to produce notes. Pedals or levers are used to adjust the pitch, allowing for chromatic changes and a broad harmonic range.

Genres

The harp is commonly used in classical music, Celtic and folk traditions, and contemporary genres like jazz and new age music. It is also featured in orchestras and solo performances.
